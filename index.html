City Profile & Economic Performance

Geography & Heritage

Ahmedabad, situated on the banks of the Sabarmati, is India's first UNESCO World Heritage City. It covers 505 sq. km and serves as the primary commercial engine of Gujarat.

Strategic Hub: Integrated into the Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC) with high-speed connectivity to Mundra and Kandla ports.

The Manchester of the East

A global leader in Textiles and Pharma, and an emerging hub for EV and Automobile clusters. It contributes approximately 25% to Gujarat's GDP.

Quality of Life: Consistently ranked among the top 3 in the Ease of Living Index (Million+ category) with a robust 88.3% literacy rate.

Critical Urban Challenges

Environmental Sustainability

Groundwater extraction is at an unsustainable 152%. The city faces an urgent requirement for reclaimed water infrastructure to reduce Narmada surface water dependence.

Waste Management

The Pirana landfill is undergoing massive bio-mining to reclaim land and reduce air/soil contamination in the surrounding urban habitation.

Policy Innovation

Sustainable Green Bond Issuance

AMC leveraged its AA+ rating to issue a ₹200 Cr Green Bond specifically for STP upgrades. This marks a benchmark shift from grant-based systems to market-based environmental financing.
